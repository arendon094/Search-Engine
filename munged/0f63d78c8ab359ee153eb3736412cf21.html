Timasitheus (disambiguation)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The name Timasitheus or Timesitheus (Gr. Τιμασίθεος or Τιμησίθεος) belonged to several people in Classical history:

  • Gaius Furius Sabinius Aquila Timesitheus, a Roman knight of the 3rd century who was the most important advisor to Gordian III
  • Timasitheus of Delphi, an ancient Greek athlete
  • Timasitheus of Trapezus, a translator from Asia Minor mentioned in Xenophon's Anabasis
  • Timasitheus of Lipara, first archon of the city of Lipara
