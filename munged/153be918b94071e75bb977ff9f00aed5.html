Negimaki

Negimaki (ねぎ巻き, Negimaki?), also called negima, is a Japanese food consisting of broiled strips of beef smothered in teriyaki sauce and rolled with scallions (negi). They are also commonly referred to as Negima, and are thought to have originated from the Kantō region of Japan.
