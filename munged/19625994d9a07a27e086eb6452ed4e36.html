Morris Ximenes

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Sir Morris Ximenes (aka Moses Ximenes) (1762 - 1837) was a captain in the British Army and Berkshire landowner who had converted to Anglicanism from Judaism

Morris was born in London about 1762. He was a member of the London Exchange, where he made a large fortune. In 1802, he was elected a warden of the Bevis Marks Synagogue, but declined to accept; and on being fined he resigned from the community and became converted to Christianity. He afterwards adopted a military career and served in the Peninsular War as Captain Ximenes. He was knighted and appointed high sheriff of both the counties of Kent and of Berkshire. His chief residence was Bear Place at Hare Hatch, near Wargrave, in the latter county. He died in London in 1837.

His younger brother, Sir David Ximenes, had no connection with the Jewish community.
