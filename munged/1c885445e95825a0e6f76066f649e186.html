M class Minesweeper (Germany)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The M class were the standard German Navy (Kriegsmarine) minesweeper of World War 2. The German Language term was Minensuchboot. They undertook many tasks for the German Navy and were respected by their opponents

[edit] History

[edit] M1935

The first series; the M1935 were ordered in the late 1930's to replace worn out World War I vintage boats. These ships proved versatile and seaworthy. The vessels could also undertake convoy escort, anti-submarine warfare and minelaying tasks as well as minesweeping. A total of 69 vessels were built between 1937 and 1941, 34 M1935 ships were sunk during the war

[edit] M1940

Although the M1935 was a very satisfactory vessel, it was complex and expensive to build and a simplified design was put into production in 1941. These ships had coal fired boilers because of oil shortages. A total of 127 ships were built between 1941 and 1944. 63 M1940 ships were sunk during the war

[edit] M1943

This was a further simplified and slightly enlarged version of the M 1940. These ships were designed for pre-fabrication and were produced in four versions

  • Minesweeper
  • anti submarine vessel with extra depth charges
  • Torpedo boat , with two 533mm torpedo tubes
  • Torpedo training vessel

Only 18 vessels were completed by the time the war ended.

[edit] Post War

After the End of the War the suriving ships were taken over by the German Minesweeping Administration and used to clear the coat of Northern Europe of mines. Some of these ships were taken up by Allied Navies, including 43 Vessels transferred to the Soviet Navy and Five vessels returned to the Bundesmarine in the late 1950's
