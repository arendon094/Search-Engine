Indeevar

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Indeevar (1924 – February 27, 1999 ) was one of the leading Hindi film lyricists in the 1960's. He grew up in Barua Sagar in Jhansi District, from where he moved to Bombay.

He got his break in Malhaar in 1956, which became a hit due to the song "Bade Armano Se Rakkha Hae Balam Teri Kasam" penned by him, and set to music by the late Roshan. He wrote over a thousand songs in over 300 films in a career spanning over four decades.

[edit] Major Hits

  • Zindagi ka safar, Hai yeh kaisa safar Koi samjha nahi … Koi jaana nahi … (Safar)
  • Bade Armaan Se Rakha Hai Balam Teri Kasam (Malhar)
  • Kasme Vaade Pyaar Wafa (Upkar)
  • Dulhan Chali, O Pehen Chali and Koi Jab Tumhara Hriday Tod De (Purab Aur Paschim)
  • Chandan Sa Badan’ and Main To Bhool Chali Babul Ka Des. (Saraswati Chandra)
  • Pal Bhar Ke Liye Koi Hame Pyaar Kar Le (Johny Mera Naam)

[edit] Awards

  • 1975 Filmfare Best Lyricist Award for Amanush film's "Dil Aisa Kisi Ne Mera Toda" sung by Kishore Kumar
