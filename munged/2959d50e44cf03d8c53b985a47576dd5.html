Diffraction spike

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Diffraction spikes seen from various stars in the Pleiades.

Diffraction spikes are lines radiating from bright light sources in reflecting telescope images. They are a kind of lens flare artifact due to light diffracting around the support vanes of the secondary mirror. Refracting telescopes and their photographic images do not have the same problem. Although diffraction spikes can obscure parts of a photograph and are undesired in professional contexts, some amateur astronomers like the visual effect they give to bright stars - the "Star of Bethlehem" appearance - and even modify their refractors to exhibit the same effect, [1] or to assist with focusing when using a Charge-coupled device [2].
