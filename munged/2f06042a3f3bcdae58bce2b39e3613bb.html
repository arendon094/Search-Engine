Agua Dulce, California

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Vasquez Rocks

Agua Dulce (Spanish for freshwater, literally sweet water) is an unincorporated community located in Los Angeles County, California just north of Santa Clarita. The town has a population of about 4,000. It is located at 34°29′30″N, 118°19′50″W and covers a geographic area of about 25 square miles (65 km²). The ZIP code is 91390 and area code 661.

[edit] Geography

Location of Agua Dulce in Los Angeles County, California.

Agua Dulce is located about 25 miles (40 km) Southwest of Palmdale, 44 miles (71 km) North of Los Angeles, in the Sierra Pelona Valley portion of Southern California.

[edit] Demographics

The town has a median household income of $78,000 (US). The average household size is 3.1.

[edit] About Agua Dulce

Agua Dulce is best known for Vasquez Rocks California State Park. Vasquez Rocks has been long used as a popular filming location by the Hollywood movie industry, most notably The Flintstones movie and the Star Trek episode "Arena." It got its name from the famous bandit Tiburcio Vasquez that used to run and hide there while being looked for.

Agua Dulce is home to the Agua Dulce Vineyards, the largest winery in Los Angeles County.

Agua Dulce also has a general aviation airport known as Agua Dulce Airpark.
