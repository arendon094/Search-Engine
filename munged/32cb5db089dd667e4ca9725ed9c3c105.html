Lee Webb (newscaster)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Lee Webb is the anchor of The 700 Club, the flagship program of The Christian Broadcasting Network (CBN), and Newswatch, a half-hour daily news program also on CBN[1]. He was born in Pompano Beach, Florida[1].

[edit] Career

After graduating from Auburn University in 1975, he anchored and reported sports for WSFA-TV in Montgomery, Alabama[1]. He worked as sports anchor for NBC affiliate WCKT (now WSVN) in Miami from 1977 to 1983, again for WCVB-TV in Boston in 1983, and weeknight news anchor for WTLV, the NBC affiliate in Jacksonville in 1986[1]. He helped found Christians in the Media, a fellowship for local journalists, and is an elder in the Presbyterian Church in America[1].

In addition, he has performed eight years of military service, including deployment to Central America and Operation Desert Shield[1]. He was a captain in the Florida Army National Guard[1].
