Memorial square

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

 This article does not cite any references or sources. (February 2007)
Please help improve this article by adding citations to reliable sources. Unverifiable material may be challenged and removed.

A memorial square is an intersection dedicated in memory of somebody, usually somebody who was killed in a war. It is not the same as a town square. While the name of a town square is used to describe where something is located, the name of a memorial square is not used this way.
