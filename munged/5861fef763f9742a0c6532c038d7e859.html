Alexander Maniatis

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alexander Richard Maniatis (born November 9, 1981) is the CEO/Owner of Dopamine Records in Boston, Massachusetts. He is a former member of Boston post-rockers Eksi Ekso (formerly On Fire) and was the former singer in the indie/post punk band RADKA. Maniatis founded indie label Amalagate Records in 2000.

[edit] Background

Alexander attended Hamilton-Wenham Regional High School in Hamilton, Massachusetts and then Emerson College, in Boston, Massachusetts. He worked as a tour manager for Matt Squire's (who later went on to produce multi-platinum selling Panic! at the Disco) band The Rise Park as well many numerous indie/pop/hardcore/punk outfits before settling back in Cambridge, Massachusetts to take over the reign at Dopamine Records.
