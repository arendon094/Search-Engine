Leptines II

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Leptines II (d. aft. 365), son of Leptines I, was the nephew of Dionysius the Elder.

In 351 BC, Leptines aided Callippus in successfully expelling the garrison of Dionysius the Younger from Rhegium. After civil unrest within the city, Leptines and Polyperchon turned on Callippus stabbing him with reputedly the same sword that killed Dion.[1]

In 342 BC, when Timoleon liberated Sicily, Leptines was sent into exile. He died in Corinth.
