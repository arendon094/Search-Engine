Colorado and Wyoming Railway

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Originally founded in 1899, the Colorado and Wyoming Railway is a subsidiary of the Oregon Steel Mills, Inc.. It hauls coal, ore and steel products on about five miles of track inside Oregon Steel's Rocky Mountain Steels Mills facility in Pueblo, Colorado, to connect with the Union Pacific Railroad and the BNSF Railroad.
