Ossoliński

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The family coat of arms was Topór.

Ossoliński (plural: Ossolińscy) is the surname of a Polish szlachta (nobility) family. Because Polish adjectives have different forms for the genders, Ossolińska is the same form for a female family member

[edit] History

The Ossoliński was a magnate family. They appeared in the historical annals at the beginning of the 14th century. The family originated from Ossolin in Lesser Poland. The progenitor of the family was Jan z Ossolina, son of Great Marshal of the Crown and castellan of Kraków Nawoja z Tęczyna. Jan was the main heir of the property that Nawoja left after his death. Due to the tradition in medieval Poland, he started to use the surname derived from the main family seat.

The Ossoliński Institute in Lwów.

Grand Chancellor of the Crown Jerzy Ossolinski was granted a hereditary princely title from Pope Urban VIII in 1633, a similar title, Reichsfürst, from the Emperor Ferdinand II in 1634. Similar title was granted to Jerzy's cousin Franciszek Maksymilian Ossolinski from Louis XV, King of France, in 1736. Both titles became extinct in 1790. His famous library that he located in Lwów the "Ossolineum", has initiated the beginning of the Ossoliński Institute, which was founded by Józef Maksymilian Ossolinski in 1817.

The Ossolinski's castle was reputably the biggest in Europe prior to the building of Versailles (see James Michener's book Poland), which name 'Krzyztopor' mean The Battle Axe of The Cross - whose descendents are represented in North Western England through Count Boris Ossolinski, whose Jackson heiress bride is remembered in England as Countess Mary Ossalinsky.

[edit] Members

  • Jozef Kajetan Ossolinski (1758-1834), castellan, senator
