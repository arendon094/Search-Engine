List of Jehovah's Witnesses Publications

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jehovah's Witnesses Publications are books, brochures, magazines and tracts published by the Watchtower Bible and Tract Society of New York for distribution by and personal use of Jehovah's Witnesses. Most of the copyrights are held by the Watch Tower Bible and Tract Society of Pennsylvania.

These publications were sold until the early 1990s; Watchtower magazine is now distributed free of charge. Donations are solicited, and have made the Watchtower Bible & Tract Society of NY, Inc. #24 in the top 40 most profitable companies in New York, reporting an annual revenue of about $951 million USD[1], free of taxation as it is registered as a non-profit corporation. The company's employees are volunteers who live and work in the Brooklyn and Pennsylvania Bethel complex.

[edit] Songboooks

Sing Praises to Jehovah - 225 Kingdom songs, with words and melodies composed entirely by dedicated servants of Jehovah from all parts of the earth. Phonograph records and audiocassettes were produced to accompany singing

  • “Songs of the Bride”—1879
144 hymns expressing the desires and hopes of Christ’s bride
  • “Poems and Hymns of Millennial Dawn”—1890
151 poems and 333 hymns, published without music. Most were the works of well-known writers
  • “Watch Tower” of February 1 was devoted to “Zion’s Glad Songs of the Morning”—1896
Words for 11 songs, with music; lyrics written by Bible Students
  • “Zion’s Glad Songs”—1900
82 songs, many of them written by one Bible Student; to supplement the earlier collection
  • “Hymns of the Millennial Dawn”—1905
The 333 songs published in 1890, but with music
  • “Kingdom Hymns”—1925
80 songs, with music, especially for children
  • “Songs of Praise to Jehovah”—1928
337 songs, a mixture of new ones written by the Bible Students and older hymns. In the lyrics, special effort was made to break away from sentiments of false religion and from creature worship
  • “Kingdom Service Song Book”—1944
62 songs. Adapted to Kingdom service needs of the times. No credits were given to authors or composers
  • “Songs to Jehovah’s Praise”—1950
91 songs. This songbook had more up-to-date themes and dispensed with archaic language. It was translated into 18 languages
  • “Singing and Accompanying Yourselves With Music in Your Hearts”—1966
119 songs covering every aspect of Christian living and worship. Music known to have originated with secular or false-religious sources was deleted. Orchestral recordings of the entire book were made and were extensively used as accompaniment in congregation meetings. Some vocal selections were also recorded. Starting in 1980, recordings of orchestral arrangements of “Kingdom Melodies” were produced so that at home individuals could enjoy music that would be upbuilding
  • Sing Praises to Jehovah—1984 (to present)
