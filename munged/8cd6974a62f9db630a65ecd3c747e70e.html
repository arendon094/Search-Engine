Adhemar

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Adhemar, Ademar, Adamar, or Aimar (French: Adémar or Adhémar, Italian: Ademaro, Ademario, Adelmario, or Adamaro, Latin: Ademarius, Occitan: Azemar or Azemars, Portuguese: Ademar) is a masculine given name, and eventually surname, which could refer to:

  • Adhemar of Salerno (died 861), prince
  • Adhemar of Capua (died after 1000), prince
  • Adémar de Chabannes (died 1034), historian
  • Adhemar of Le Puy (died 1098), bishop
  • Aimar V of Limoges (died c. 1199), viscount
  • Ademar lo Negre (fl. 1219), troubadour
  • Adhémar Jean Claude Barré de Saint-Venant (died 1886), mechanician
  • Adhémar Raynault (died 1984), politician
  • Adhemar da Silva (died 2001), athlete
  • Adhemar Pimenta (fl. 1942), sports manager
  • Adhemar de Chaunac (fl. 1961), vintner
  • Adhemar Ferreira de Camargo Neto (1972–present), athlete
  • Ademar dos Santos Batista (1983–present), athlete
  • Ademar Aparecido Xavier Júnior (1985–present), athlete
  • Ademar Braga (–present), sports manager

Adhémar or Ademar could also form a compound name:

  • Guilhem Ademar (fl. 1217), troubadour
  • Louis-Alfred-Adhémar Rivet (died 1951), politician

Adhemar or Aimar could also be a surname:

  • Joseph Adhemar (died 1862), mathematician
  • Pablo Aimar (1979–present), footballer

Adhemar, Adhémar, or Ademar could also refer to

  • La Garde-Adhémar
  • Château des Adhémar
  • Bronzen Adhemar
  • CB Ademar León
  • Rodovia Adhemar de Barros

For another meaning of Adamar, see Ramada.
