Talaat Moustafa

Talaat Moustafa (Arabic: طلعت مصطفى )June 6, 1926-February 28, 2005 (died at 78) was chairman and founder of The Talaat Moustafa Group.

Talaat Moustafa was one of the personalities who put in effort to help and aid Egyptians. He helped fight poverty in Egypt and helped the poor with their problems. For instance, before his death he opened the Talaat Moustafa philanthropy hospital.

The Talaat Moustafa Group is one of the biggest companies in Egypt today. The group has a total of 23 companies and employes more than 10,000 people. It has a construction branch which is managed and looked after by his eldest son, Tarek Talaat Moustafa, who is a member of the Egyptian Parliament. Also, part of The Talaat Moustafa Group is an agriculture branch which is managed and looked after by his second eldest son, Hani Talaat Moustafa. The last, but certainly not least, major branch in The Talaat Moustafa Group is the real estate branch which is managed and looked after by his youngest son and member of the Egyptian senate, Hisham Talaat Moustafa. The Talaat Moustafa groups owns the biggest resort in Egypt, El-Rehab, and other major resorts. TMG has opened three Four Seasons Hotels in Egypt and is working on a major resort in Marsa Alam. Recently, in July 2006, The Talaat Moustafa Group started on the Madinaty project which occupies 8,000 feddans. Madinaty will be the biggest all inclusive city in the Middle East.
