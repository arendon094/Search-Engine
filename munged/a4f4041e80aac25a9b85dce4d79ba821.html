Gorka Iraizoz Moreno (born March 6, 1981 in Pamplona, Navarre) is a Spanish footballer. He plays as a goalkeeper for Athletic Bilbao in the Spanish La Liga.

He has previously played for also played for Chantrea, Gernika, SD Eibar and Espanyol.

Arriving in Espanyol in July 2005, he was the squad's second choice-goalkeeper behind Carlos Kameni for the 2006-07 league season, but was the regular in the club's brilliant UEFA Cup run. Despite being from Navarre he has learned to speak fluent Catalan.

On the August 8, 2007 he signed for Athletic Bilbao for 4.6 million euros, returning to the club he spent one and a half season with, from 1999 to 2001, this time as the undisputed starter. However, due to a severe injury he was left out of the squad for the second part of the season. His place (and shirt number) was taken by 37-year old Armando, signed from Cádiz CF until the end of the 2007-08 season.

[edit] Honours

  • Spanish Cup winner in 2006 with RCD Espanyol
  • UEFA Cup runner-up in 2007 with RCD Espanyol
