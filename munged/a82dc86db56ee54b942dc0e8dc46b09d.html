Aleksandr Samokhvalov

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aleksandr Andreyevich Samokhvalov (1894-1974) was a Russian painter and graphic artist.

He trained at the Academy of Arts for achitecture St. Petersburg from 1914 to 1917 and in Kuzma Petrov-Vodkin’s workshop from 1920 to 1923. He travelled with Petrov-Vodkin to Samarkand. In 1924 Samokhvalov designed a memorial to Lenin in Leningrad in the form of a gigantic glass cube.
