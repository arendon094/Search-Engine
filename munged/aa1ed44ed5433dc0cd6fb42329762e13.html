Dream of a Lifetime was the first posthumously released compilation of music by American soul singer Marvin Gaye. Released in 1985, it featured a mixture of songs Gaye had worked on both before his death at the hands of his father on April 1, 1984, and from the archives of Motown during Gaye's late-1970s tenure with the label.

The recently recorded "Sanctified Lady" (originally titled "Sanctified Pussy") was completed by Gordon Banks and released as a single, reaching #2 on the US R&B charts. A second single, "It's Madness", peaked at a more modest #55 on the same singles chart and would be Gaye's last single until "My Last Chance" six years later.

Dream of a Lifetime peaked at #8 on the R&B album chart and #41 on the pop album chart upon its release.
